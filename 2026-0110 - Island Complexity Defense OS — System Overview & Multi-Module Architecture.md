## 01 â€” Problem Statement

Small, mountainous islands face a structural configuration:

- Geographically **exposed yet compact**  
- Economically **dense yet resource-constrained**  
- Militarily **vulnerable in classical metrics** (platform counts, range, mass)

Conventional responses attempt to:

- Imitate great-power force structures

- **Asymmetry Mismatch**

- **Civilâ€“Defense Disconnect**  
  Defensive measures are bolted on as separate, unpopular layers; citizens do not internalize or naturally use resilience structures.

- **Precision-Age Exposure**  
  Clean, efficiently organized environments become **highly targetable**, with predictable patterns and low entropy.

- **Underused Natural Advantages**  
  Mountains, complex coasts, dense cities, underground spaces, and local knowledge remain **under-formalized** as strategic assets.

The missing piece is an architecture that:

1. Treats **complexity as the primary defensive resource**, not a side effect.
