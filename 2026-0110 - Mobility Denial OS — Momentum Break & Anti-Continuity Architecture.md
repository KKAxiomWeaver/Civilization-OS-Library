### 2.2 Core Concepts

## 03 â€” Mechanicsï¼ˆHow It Worksï¼‰

### 3.1 Momentum Field Model

For any region R, MD-OS computes:

- **MF_E(R)** â€” External Momentum Field:
  - Evaluated assuming external actor chooses routes by:
    - Shortest time,  
    - Most continuous width,  
    - Lowest geometric complexity.

- **MF_H(R)** â€” Home Momentum Field:
  - Evaluated along IGM/DGM (protected internal routes).

Goals:

- Keep **MF_E** low (hard to maintain speed & coherence).  
- Keep **MF_H** sufficiently high in IGM (easy enough for home actors).

MF is affected by:

- Slope, curvature, width, surface type, urban geometry, signal & visibility.

### 3.2 External Mobility Manifold (EMM) vs IGM

- **EMM** is inferred by:
  - Running external-style routing over the islandâ€™s graph:
    - â€œIf I knew nothing and wanted fastest path from A to B, what would I choose?â€

- MD-OS then:

  - Identifies segments where:
    - EMM overlaps with IGM in undesirable ways (shared fast lanes).  
    - EMM offers clean, low-breakpoint paths.

  - Proposes:
    - Geometric adjustments,  
    - Zoning / design changes,  
    - Where necessary, insertion of ACNs and BPs.

Result:

> External fastest paths become  
> **slow, broken, many-choice, coordination-hostile paths**.

### 3.3 Breakpoints (BP)

Breakpoints are intentionally designed or leveraged features:

- **Geometric BPs**  
  - Sharp turns, chicanes, S-curves.  
  - Changes in width (from wide to narrow to wide).  
  - Vertical undulations (ramps, small climbs/descents).

- **Environmental BPs**  
  - Street trees, pillars, columns that break line-of-sight and wide arcs.  
  - Terrain exposures that penalize certain formations.

- **Functional BPs**  
  - Intersection zones with multiple branching options.  
  - Mixed-use spaces where movement must slow (markets, shared spaces).

MD-OS ensures:

- EMM paths encounter BPs at regular intervals.  
- IGM paths either:
  - Avoid BPs (if they cause too much delay for H), or  
  - Use BPs that are manageable under OS support (e.g., known chokes with civil flow micro-design).

### 3.4 Anti-Continuity Nodes (ACN)

ACNs are junctions/segments designed to:

- Break a long straight run into **segments** with:

  - Different radii of curvature,  
  - Shifts between ground & elevated levels,  
  - Multiple plausible follow-on options.

Properties:

- For external actors:
  - Harder to pre-plan large formations through them without slowdown.  
  - High vulnerability to miscoordination & congestion.

- For home actors:
  - ACNs can be:
    - Known landmarks in IGM,  
    - Backed by Micro-Hubs and UG Spine nodes,  
    - Sites with good signage & local control in crises.

### 3.5 Mobility Denial via Space, Not Destruction

MD-OS emphasizes:

- **Design & configuration** of space, not its destruction:

  - Bridges remain; theyâ€™re just not easy â€œaircraft carrier runways on landâ€.  
  - Highways exist; but they have:

    - Carefully placed BPs & ACNs,  
    - Off-ramps into complex webs,  
    - Restrictive design for large hostile formations.

- **Resilience Mesh & Civil Flow** ensure:
  - Home mobility remains viable even as external momentum is broken.

  - Reclassifies the valley as:

  - Routes residents via alternate hillside corridors or underground segments.

### 5.3 Urban Grid & Roundabout ACNs

- In urban districts:
  - MD-OS favors:
    - Shorter blocks,  
    - More intersections,  
    - Roundabouts or multi-branch nodes.

  - Cannot maintain high speed long;  
  - Must constantly choose paths, suffering coordination friction.

- IGM:
  - Pre-selected safe-grid alignments for H, with signage & semantics.

### 5.4 Bridge & Causeway Design

- Bridges connecting coastal areas to city / HF:

  - MD-OS avoids:
    - Long, flat, wide, dead-straight causeways.

  - Favors:
    - Multi-span segments with BPs (curves, branching points),  
    - Option for local-only branches & bypasses.

- Reduces external ability to â€œroll straight inâ€.  
- Still supports everyday local traffic.

## 06 â€” Risks & Limitations

Mobility Denial OS must be applied carefully:

- **Economic Efficiency Trade-offs**  
  Over-curving or over-breaking may raise logistics costs for everyday commerce.

- **Emergency Response Balance**  
  Too much movement friction can also slow emergency services if IGM is poorly designed.

- **Political & Social Acceptability**  
  People and businesses may resist design choices that reduce straight, high-speed routes.

- **Misapplication Risks**  
  Applying MD-OS logic everywhere may create:
  - Congested, confusing cities,  
  - With lower quality of life.

MD-OS explicitly avoids:

- Designing streets solely to â€œtrapâ€ outsiders at the cost of localsâ€™ daily usability.  
- Encouraging blockages that impede evacuation or emergency response.  
- Replacing traditional traffic safety/efficiency thinking; it is an additional layer.
